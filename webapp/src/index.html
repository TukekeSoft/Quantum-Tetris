Tetris on a Quantum Computer

We searched for fun video games in the quantum space and found nothing but chess and puzzles. So we built Quantum Tetris, and made it the first web-hosted game to run on a real Quantum Computer. Confused by Quantum? Check out this video by Kurzgesagt and the Australian Academy of Sciences:

How to Play

Click section headers to learn more about the topic.

Basic Tetris Rules

Quantum Tetris plays just like regular tetris! Blocks fall one at a time, it's up to you to rotate and position them. Fill a full row to clear it. If blocks reach the screen top, then it's Game Over!

Quantum Superposition Pieces

Superposition pieces exist in two states at once! While falling, they flip back and forth between piece types based on their quantum probability. The only way to force a superposition piece to settle in one state is to 'measure' the piece by placing it down on the tetris stack.

Controls

Position pieces with LEFT and RIGHT arrow keys. Rotate pieces with Z and X. Hold the DOWN arrow to fast fall, and use SPACEBAR to instant drop.

Entangled Pieces

Entangled pieces are connected with an invisibe quantum tether. When one entangled piece changes state, it affects the other as well no matter how far they are apart! When one entangled piece has a block cleared, the corresponding block on its partner piece disappears as well.
